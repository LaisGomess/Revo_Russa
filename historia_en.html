Historical summary (1917)

The Russian Revolution of 1917 includes a chain of events — notably the February uprising, leading to Tsar Nicholas II's abdication, and the October insurrection that brought the Bolsheviks to power. We combine scholarly clarity with visitor-friendly narration: clear dates, social causes and the locations where events unfolded.

  • February 1917: Strikes and protests in Petrograd; collapse of the czarist regime.
  • October 1917: Seizure of strategic points; provisional government overthrown.
  • Consequences: Russia exits WWI and enters the Civil War (1918–1921).

Tours foreground social context (shortages, women's mobilization, role of soviets) and include referenced materials for curious visitors and students.
